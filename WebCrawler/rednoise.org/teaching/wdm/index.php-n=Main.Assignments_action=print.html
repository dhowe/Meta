"If the artist carries through his idea and makes it into visible form, then all the steps in the process are of importance... All intervening steps --scribbles, sketches, drawings, failed works, models, studies, thoughts, conversations-- are of interest. Those that show the thought process of the artist are sometimes more interesting than the final product." - Sol Lewitt


Assignment #1 (due Jan 20, 12pm)


1. Create a website (or blog) for the course and send me the link via email (if the page has a password, be sure to include it). This page will eventually hold links to ALL OF YOUR WORK for the course, so design it with this in mind. The top of your first page MUST include:

  • The course name and number
  • Your name (and nickname if you have one)
  • Your student ID #
  • Your CityU email address
  • Clear links to each assignment

2. Read this set of 17 very short fiction pieces (1 line + 1 image). For more inspiration, continue to the slightly longer ones here. Pick ONE that you particularly like and be prepared in class to discuss why.

3. Write a VERY short piece of fiction, prose, or poetry in English (similar to those above), and render it (in p5.js) over an image. Link your work (with a TITLE) at the top of the page you created above.

Goal: Make your display choices (color, font, spacing, motion, sound, etc.) reflect the content of your writing in some way. Do not try to create a meme; the goal is to convey (or suggest) a meaningful story with minimal language. Let the words you choose suggest a larger, more complex chain of events.
