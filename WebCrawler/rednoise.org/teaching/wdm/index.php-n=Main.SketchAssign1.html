Self Portrait
Fiona Fone

All of the audio files in this cellphone were found in documents folders and iTunes on my two-year old macbook. "o" is my high school best friend covering Def Leppard's "Pour Some Sugar On Me." "e" is a fraction of a real voicemail from my boss. "r" is a live stream of the student radio station I manage. Many of the files were accidentally uploaded to my computer when I borrowed a digital audio recorder from a friend, and a lot of them were just taking up space in various folders, usually with a filename like 080514-006.mp3

The telephone framework is not to suggest that I'm especially fond of my cellphone - I'm not, actually, much for phone calls - but rather to evoke a social outreach, and to frame each auditory moment through my relationship to someone or something else. Some of the clips start slow, some are brash and immediate. Some represent real moments with friends and family, and at least one at any time represents copyrighted content.

     Angela Ferraiolo

     Claire Kwong

Processing
Processing v2

(Click on the applet and type.)

This piece attempts to convey the frustration I encountered both while writing my poem and learning Processing. It calls attention to the artificial nature of the constraints of this assignment and the Processing environment. The “interactions window” parodies error messages that I encounter while programming - they are literally expressing my incompetence as a programmer and a writer. Finally, the look of the applet and the web page it’s situated on creates a trompe-d’oeil by simulating the interface of a desktop, thus immersing the user in my experience.

     Jak Koning

Self Portrait
Self Portrait # 2

At first, the piece was simple code to get a strong result. Using controlled randomness, the initial static that appeared became an image. The transparency of my name allowed for me get an almost ghostly image to be created. Unfortunately, the idea could not be easily transfered to include interactivity that would impart meaning to the sketch. I then decided to play with some other Processing libraries and turn it more into an exploration of the what I could code. Using Traer physics, I turned my names into small particles that when gathered and released, explode. I feel both pieces could easily represent anyone with very minor tweaks to the code. In later iterations, if they exist, I would try to make the sketch more meaningful to me personally.

     Miranda Steele

Fill in the Blanks
dear diar_

This piece is intended to model the process of getting to know someone. I took a diary entry of mine and split it into twelve lines, each of which was assigned to a different person to read aloud. At first, the only letters revealed in each line are those of my initials (m. k. s.) and the speakers are flustered and awkward. When the UP key is pressed, more letters are added (now, miranda k.s.) and the speakers read their lines again, beginning to guess at the meanings of the words in the diary entry. When the UP key is pressed again, all possible letters in my full name are shown (miranda katherine steele) and the readers are much more comfortable saying their lines. The process of the readers learning and becoming comfortable with their lines mirrors the audience's role in getting to know me, through reading the increasingly legible diary entry.

     Lucia Lopez

My DNA

     G. G.

"Hello, World!" is an interactive piece that aims to define a thing via external, rather than internal, features. We are all reflections, to one extent or another, of the world we see. Sometimes, the world we see is also a reflection of ourselves. It seems that there are different ways we might see ourselves in the world. If we are concrete, we see ourselves in a stable, clear way. On the other hand, if we are more abstract, we see ourselves in a more random, vague way. Perhaps there is some equilibrium at which we are most in-tune with our world.

Self Portrait
FOCUS: Self Portrait Round 2

I wanted this piece to convey the difficulty I sometimes have simultaneously focusing on all of the different aspects of my life. I have a lot going on, and doing a good job on one thing often means losing sight of the "big picture." To that end, the jumbled letters of my name are constantly sorting themselves around on the screen. They do a better job the closer they are to the cursor. The unpleasant high-pitched noise in the background has volume proportional to the overall sortedness of all of the letters. In other words, the noise dims when the user is successfully able to multitask.

     Diem Tran

Self Portrait 1

This sketch represents me, as most of the world population, am only a flash of light through the universe. We are different, but we all appear and disappear after a while, as words are flashing in the sketch. People do have pride, a courage for them to live. So am I. An enough intensity of pride helps me live, study, work and enjoy, just like the center word growing, blinking and stop growing.

Self Portrait 2

The white word "DT" following the mouse represents me, a normal person among everyone else. It's a rule of life that people gather and separate. Every gathering brings ups and downs of life, no single union is repeated, that is the Melody of the world. I want to be a part of life, to contribute to the Melody of this world.
