MiniProject: Generating with Grammars


  • Prepare a work for class that uses your own context-free grammar. Find a way to use this technique to make something creative, expressive and conceptually engaging.
  • The goal here is not only to understand the workings of grammars and their potential, but to create a successful artistic work. Consider (and be able to articulate) how each choice you make (from size to font to color to motion, etc.) contributes to your goals for the piece. As mentioned in class, one potentially interesting strategy is to try to create juxtapositions between text and image (or audio, video, whatever...)
  • As grammars are often used as a more expressive version of simple substitution, you may want to begin by writing 'chunks' of text that can later be included in your grammar (Note: this is only 1 suggestion for how to get started...)
  • Remember that you can validate your grammar structure with JSON-lint.


Constraints
  • The sketch must use some form of spoken/written language as a basic content unit (this can include drawn letters, phonemes, words, including images of text, or recorded voice samples). While the obvious choice may be to generate strings of text from your grammar, this is not the only option.
  • Primary content (from text, to drawing commands, to image files, to sound samples, etc.) should be sequenced and/or generated from a grammar.
