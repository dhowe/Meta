Syllabus

DESCRIPTION

A project-oriented course for creative writers and coders interested in integrating text and language into their computational art practice. Lectures will address aesthetic, conceptual, and technical concerns arising at the intersection of creative coding and textual art practice. Projects may include everything from hypertext to kinetic poetry, to networked narratives, to texts authored wholly or in part by computer program, to physically-situated installations and beyond. Lab time will focus on developing programming skills required for aesthetic expression in textual digital media using free and open-source tools. Prerequisite: Programming Fundamentals, CMS3, or equivalent experience with at least one modern programming language: C/C++, Java (Processing), JavaScript, Ruby, Python, etc.

REQUIREMENTS

Students are expected to arrive on time to all sessions, complete weekly assignments, and contribute to class critiques and discussion. Students will put together an online portfolio to showcase their work, and complete a final project. Please be on time. If you are more than fifteen minutes late, you will be considered absent. If you have to miss class for any reason, let me know, and be sure to check with me or your classmates for work you may have missed. More than one unexcused absence and you risk failing the class. Make sure you back up your work - lost or corrupted files do not excuse you from handing in assignments.

COURSE TOPICS

  • Introduction: Word, Image, Sound
  • Concrete Poetry & Kinetic Text
  • Harvesting Language
  • Language and Probability
  • Generating with Grammars
  • Slicing & Dicing with Regular Expressions
  • Form, Constraints & the Oulipo
  • Appropriation, Copyright & Uncreative Writing
  • Translation & its Discontents

READINGS (selections)

  • Wardrip-Fruin, Noah, and Nick Monfort, eds. The New Media Reader.
  • Aarseth, Espen. Cybertext: Perspectives on Ergodic Literature. Baltimore and London: Johns Hopkins University Press, 1997.
  • Funkhouser, Christopher T. Prehistoric Digital Poetry: An Archaeology of Forms, 1959-1995. Tuscaloosa: University of Alabama Press, 2007. 
  • Ricardo, Francisco J. Literary Art in Digital Performance: Case Studies in New Media Art and Criticism. New York: Continuum, 2009.
  • Hayles, N. Katherine. Electronic Literature: New Horizons for the Literary. Notre Dame, University of Notre Dame Press, 2008.
  • Cayley, John. "Writing (under-)Sky: On Xu Bing's 'Tianshu'." A * Book of the Book: Some Work and Projections About the Book and Writing . Eds. Jerome Rothenberg and Stephen Clay. New York: Granary Books, 2000. 497-503.

KEYWORDS

Writing, Coding, Literature, Language, Text, Copyright, Censorship, Privacy, Algorithms, Networks, Open Source, Hacking, Transparency, Social Media, Appropriation, Electronic Literature, Digital Poetry, Generative Language.
