Assigment #6 (Due 4/3)

Reading

-- Listen to Jackson Mac Low on LINEBREAK [30min]

-- Read 'Notes toward a reading of Jackson Mac Low' (here). Make sure you understand how Mac Low's acrostic/diastic processes work; specifically focus on 'ASYMMETRY' (see writing assignment below).

Writing

-- Write a short (1-3 page) response to the experience of writing with context-free grammars. We talked a bit about this in class, but focus here on ways this might (or might not) affect your writing/reading practice... For example, did you find it important to see the grammars that people used to create their texts; do you consider this part of the 'text'(process vs. product)? How might this affect your reading of other authors; both those who discuss/present their processes (Mac Low or Calvino, for example) and those who don't? etc.

Send these to me in advance of class, (by monday, 3/26). We may, time-permitting, read these aloud in class.

-- [Optional] Write a creative piece using Mac Low's acrostic 'ASYMMETRY' process desribed here; the example given is called 'ASSYMETRY 204'

Programming

-- There will be individual assignments for this (2-week) period focusing on gaining a better understanding of (and learning to debug) the code you have already written. These will follow in individual emails and will also be due on Tuesday, 4/3.

-- Make sure you understand how word-level 'N-Grams' work, described by Hartman (for individual letters) in the Travesty section (ch 5). Your next assignment will be to write an N-Gram based piece, so start to think about texts that might be interesting to use as source material.
