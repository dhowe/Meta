Assigment #7 (Due 4/10)

Reading

-- To press forth in our quest (never) to understand the true nature of 'writing', we will read these two (quite short) articles for next week:

'BioPoetry' by Eduardo Kac
'Sentences on Conceptual Writing?' by Kenny Goldsmith

-- Also, re-read the short section on N-grams in Hartmans chapter 5 (he calls his program 'Travesty'). Make sure you understand how word-level 'N-Grams' work. Your next assignment will be to write an N-Gram based piece, so continue to think about texts that might be interesting to use as source material. Be prepared to present your ideas for an N-gram sketch in class.

Writing

-- Write up 3 ideas for a final project. These can be variations on a single theme or 3 completely separate projects from which you will choose. Include sketches, stroy boards, images, and sounds when possible. Try and think 'out of the box'... Though we've focused on processing thus far, this doesn't imply you can't do something totally different; say a multi-channel sound piece with no visual component. Primary criteria are originality, cohesion, and communication -- make these works say something both personal & resonant to an audience. Be prepared to read/show your proposals in class and to give feedback on those of others.

Programming

-- Download and test the new pText (version-013) and play around with the N-Gram example here, adding one or more of your own texts: http://www.rednoise.org/e-writing/uploads/PMarkovExample/

-- If you have not given me your completed 'personal' programming assignment (due 4/3) pls. do so ASAP.
