An example algorithm for composing 10-lines from 10-sources

  1. Start with a sentence of your own choosing with at least 4 words, e.g., "The young man crossed the road" (source #1)
  2. Use the last 3 words of the line as a (quoted) Google search, e.g., "crossed the road"
  3. From the web pages returned, select a page to visit and copy the subsequent words as the next line, e.g., "because it was time for a change"
  4. If you like the line you've just written as an addition to the piece, note your source, e.g., http://danieledwards.org/2008/10/09/why-do-you-think-he-crossed-the-road/ and continue. If not, remove it and go back to step #3.
  5. Repeat steps 2-5 until you have 10 lines
