Assigment #3 (Due 9/28)

1. Coding
Prepare the 1st iteration of your Grammar Mini-Projects to be presented at our next class mtg. See the Haiku example for a simple use of Grammars (the work shown in class should be significantly more developed).
2. Reading
Read the intro & chapters 1-2 of Charles Hartman's 'Virtual Muse'.
Read the material on Grammars and prepare at least 1 question to ask in the lecture. This can be something that is unclear, or a potential limitation of the method itself (e.g., 'it seems impossible to do X, is that true?'). Note that the RiTa grammar object, RiGrammar, contains several extensions beyond what the typical CFG is capable of.
3. Writing
Note that CFGs are often used as a more expressive version of simple recombination so you may want to begin by writing 'chunks' of text that can later be included in your grammar (Note: this is only 1 suggestion for how to get started...)
Start to think about (and take notes on) ideas for your final project...
