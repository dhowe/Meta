Presentation

  • Engage with the artist and teach us something important about at least two of their works (involving text/language/reading/writing) -- do not just simply present the basic information from a web search. Tell us about the artist's history: other works by the artist, related works by other artists, influences, inspirations, etc. You must also address the following 3 areas:
    • Conceptual - What ideas is the artist concerned with? Why is their work considered "language-art"; how does it use &/or interrogate language? Why it is interesting and/or important?
    • Aesthetic - How does their work look? How do specific design elements and aesthetic choices relate to the core concepts.
    • Technical - How do their pieces work? What are the different components and how do they communicate. What hardware, software, and algorithms are used?
  • Note: DO NOT READ YOUR SLIDES -- this is a poor presentation technique and will be penalized.
